<p><p>The automotive engine fastener market consists of three main types: bolts, nuts, and screws. Bolts are threaded fasteners used to hold two or more components together through a pre-drilled hole. Nuts are complementary fasteners that are threaded onto bolts to secure them in place. Screws are similar to bolts, but they typically do not require a pre-drilled hole and can tap their own threads. All three types play a crucial role in the assembly and stability of automotive engines.</p></p>
