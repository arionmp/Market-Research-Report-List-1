<p><ul><li>Passive Type</li><li>Active Type</li><li>Interactive Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Self-ligating brackets are a type of orthodontic braces that do not require the use of elastic or metal ties to hold the archwire in place. They are classified into three market types: passive, active, and interactive. </p><p>Passive self-ligating brackets have a built-in clip that holds the archwire, reducing friction and allowing for smoother tooth movement. </p><p>Active self-ligating brackets have a smaller clip that actively engages the archwire, providing more control over tooth movement and allowing for faster treatment.</p><p>Interactive self-ligating brackets feature a passive or active mechanism that can be adjusted throughout the treatment, allowing for customized tooth movement and enhanced treatment efficiency.</p></p>
