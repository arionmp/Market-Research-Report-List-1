<p><p>Electronic ceramics are a diverse group of materials with applications in various industries. In the Ferroelectric market, these ceramics exhibit spontaneous polarization, making them suitable for memory devices and infrared detectors. Piezoelectric ceramics generate electric charges in response to applied mechanical stress, hence find applications in sensors, actuators, and transducers. Pyroelectric ceramics generate electric charges when exposed to temperature changes, making them ideal for infrared sensors and security systems. These different types of electronic ceramics offer unique properties and are essential for the development of technologically advanced products.</p></p>
