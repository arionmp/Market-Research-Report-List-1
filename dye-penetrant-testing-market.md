<p><p>Dye Penetrant Testing (DPT), also known as Liquid Penetrant Testing or Fluorescent Penetrant Inspection, is a widely used nondestructive testing (NDT) technique to detect surface-breaking defects in materials such as metals, ceramics, plastics, and composites. It is often used in industries such as aerospace, automotive, oil and gas, and manufacturing to ensure the quality and integrity of components and structures.</p><p>DPT involves the application of a liquid solution or dye onto the surface of the material being tested. The solution penetrates any surface defects through capillary action. After a specified dwell time, excess solution is removed, and a developer is applied to draw out the penetrant from the defects. The defects are then visible under normal or ultraviolet light, allowing technicians to identify and evaluate their size, shape, and location.</p><p>The Dye Penetrant Testing Market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period.
