<p><p>Atherosclerosis therapeutics market consists of two main types of treatments: small molecules and biologics. Small molecules are synthetic compounds that are chemically synthesized and can be administered orally. They work by targeting specific pathways or molecules involved in atherosclerosis progression. On the other hand, biologics are protein-based drugs that are derived from living organisms. They are usually administered through injections and act by inhibiting or neutralizing specific proteins or immune cells involved in atherosclerosis development. Both small molecules and biologics aim to reduce plaque buildup, prevent blood clots, and ultimately improve cardiovascular health.</p></p>
