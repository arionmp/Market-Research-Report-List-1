<p><p>The wrapping tissue market can be divided into three main types: virgin pulp, de-inked pulp, and mixed pulp. Virgin pulp refers to tissue made from fresh wood fibers, which ensures high quality and strength. De-inked pulp, on the other hand, is made from recycled paper that has been de-inked and processed into tissue. This is a more sustainable option but may have slightly lower quality. Mixed pulp is a combination of both virgin and recycled fibers, striking a balance between quality and sustainability. These different types cater to varying customer preferences and environmental concerns.</p></p>
