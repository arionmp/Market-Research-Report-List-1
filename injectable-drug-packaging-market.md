<p><p>The injectable drug packaging market consists of various types such as ampules, vials, cartridges, and bottles. Ampules are small glass containers sealed with a breakable cap used for holding a single dose of medication. Vials are larger glass or plastic containers with a rubber stopper, facilitating multiple doses. Cartridges are glass or plastic cylinders designed for single-use injection devices. Bottles are typically made of glass or plastic and are used for multi-dose medication. All these packaging types provide a means to store and administer injectable drugs safely and efficiently.</p></p>
