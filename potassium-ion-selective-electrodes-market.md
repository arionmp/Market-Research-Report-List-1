<p><p>Potassium ion selective electrodes are electrochemical sensors used to measure the concentration of potassium ions in various solutions. There are two main types of potassium ion selective electrodes: liquid membrane and PVC membrane. Liquid membrane electrodes use an organic liquid phase to selectively react with potassium ions, while PVC membrane electrodes use a polyvinyl chloride membrane that selectively binds with potassium ions. Both types of electrodes offer accurate potassium ion measurements, with liquid membrane electrodes being more suitable for complex samples and PVC membrane electrodes being more cost-effective for routine measurements.</p></p>
