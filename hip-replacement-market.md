<p><p>Hip replacement, also known as total hip arthroplasty, is a surgical procedure that involves replacing a damaged or diseased hip joint with an artificial implant or prosthesis. Additionally, advancements in implant materials, surgical techniques, and better post-operative care have contributed to the market expansion. Moreover, integration of technology, such as 3D printing, in implant manufacturing, and the rise of outpatient or same-day hip replacement surgeries are emerging trends in the market. These advancements aim to improve surgical outcomes, shorten hospital stays, and reduce the overall recovery period for patients.</p></p>

<p><p>The hip replacement market consists of several types of implants. Total hip replacement implants involve replacing the entire hip joint, while partial hip replacement implants replace only the damaged portion. Hip resurfacing implants involve reshaping the damaged hip socket and capping it with a metal prosthesis. Revision hip replacement implants are used to replace previously implanted artificial hips that have failed or worn out. These different types cater to various patient needs and offer different levels of surgical intervention and durability.</p></p>
