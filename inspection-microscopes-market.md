Two common types of inspection microscopes are binocular microscopes and trinocular microscopes. A binocular microscope has two eyepieces, allowing two eyes to view the specimen simultaneously, providing a three-dimensional image. A trinocular microscope, on the other hand, has an additional third eyepiece, which can be used for imaging or photography purposes. Both types of microscopes offer high magnification and are valuable in inspections, quality control, and research applications.</p></p>
