<p><p>Superdisintegrants are substances used in pharmaceutical formulations to enhance the disintegration and dissolution properties of solid dosage forms, such as tablets and capsules. Four primary types of superdisintegrants include sodium starch glycolate (SSG), crospovidone (XP), croscarmellose sodium (CCS), and low-substituted hydroxypropyl cellulose (L-HPC). SSG swells rapidly when in contact with water, XP absorbs water and swells to disintegrate the tablet, CCS undergoes a rapid volumetric expansion upon exposure to water, and L-HPC rapidly delivers a disintegration force. Other superdisintegrants have unique properties and mechanisms of action contributing to improved drug release and absorption.</p></p>
