<p><p>Electronic and pneumatic tourniquets are medical devices used to temporarily stop blood flow in a specific area of the body during surgeries or medical procedures. Electronic tourniquets make use of a computerized control system to regulate pressure, while pneumatic tourniquets rely on compressed air to control the pressure. Both types of tourniquets are effective in restricting blood flow and maintaining a bloodless surgical field. The choice between electronic and pneumatic tourniquets typically depends on the surgeon's preference and the specific requirements of the procedure.</p></p>
