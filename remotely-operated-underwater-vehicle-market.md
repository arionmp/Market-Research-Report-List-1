<p><p>Remotely Operated Underwater Vehicles (ROVs) can be classified into two main market types: Intervention and Observation. Intervention ROVs are designed for performing tasks such as maintenance, repairs, and construction in underwater environments. They are equipped with specialized tools and manipulators to facilitate operations. On the other hand, Observation ROVs are mainly used for gathering visual and sensor data in underwater inspections, exploration, and research. These vehicles are fitted with cameras, sensors, and lights to capture high-resolution images and videos, providing valuable information about underwater environments without directly interacting with them.</p></p>
