<p><p>The flavor system market is broadly categorized into three types: nature-identical flavors, artificial flavors, and natural flavors. Nature-identical flavors are chemically synthesized to imitate the natural flavor compounds found in a particular food or beverage. Artificial flavors are completely synthetic, created by using chemicals that do not exist in nature. Natural flavors, on the other hand, are derived from natural sources like fruits, vegetables, or spices. These different types of flavors offer various options for food and beverage manufacturers to enhance taste and aroma in their products.</p></p>
