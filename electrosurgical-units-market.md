<p><p>Electrosurgical units, used in surgical procedures, can be classified into three types: radio frequency, ultrasonic, and molecular resonance markets. Radio frequency units utilize high-frequency electrical currents to cut or coagulate tissues. Ultrasonic units, on the other hand, employ high-frequency sound waves to produce heat and vibration for tissue dissection. Lastly, molecular resonance units utilize electromagnetic energy to cause molecular oscillation and generate heat, which aids in tissue cutting. These different types of electrosurgical units serve various purposes in the medical field and contribute to improved surgical outcomes.</p></p>
