<p><p>Gas Insulated Substation (GIS) refers to a type of substation where the electrical components are enclosed in a gas-tight environment, typically using sulfur hexafluoride (SF6) gas as an insulation medium. The market for GIS can be classified into High Voltage (HV) and Ultra High Voltage (UHV) categories, based on the voltage levels they are designed for. HV GIS is intended for voltage levels between 72.5 kV and 550 kV, while UHV GIS is designed for voltage levels above 550 kV. These substation types offer compactness, improved reliability, and high resistance to environmental factors.</p></p>
