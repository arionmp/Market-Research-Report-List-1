<p><ul><li>Non-polarizing Plate Beamsplitters</li><li>Non-polarizing Cube Beamsplitters</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The non-polarizing beamsplitters market includes two types: non-polarizing plate beamsplitters and non-polarizing cube beamsplitters. Non-polarizing plate beamsplitters are thin glass plates with a specialized coating that divides an incident beam of light into two separate beams, typically with equal intensity. Non-polarizing cube beamsplitters, on the other hand, consist of a pair of prisms cemented together. They are designed to split an incoming beam of light into two paths and are commonly used in applications such as interferometry, microscopy, and spectroscopy.</p></p>
