<p><p>NanoCrystalline Cellulose (NCC) market includes three main types - Nanofibrillated Cellulose (NFC), Nanocrystalline Cellulose (NCC), and Bacterial Nanocellulose (BNC). NFC is derived from wood pulp and has high aspect ratio fibers, NCC is produced from acid hydrolysis of cellulose fibers resulting in crystalline particles, and BNC is obtained from bacterial fermentation. These materials find applications in industries such as packaging, automotive, healthcare, and electronics due to their lightweight, high strength, and eco-friendly properties.</p></p>
