<p><p>Temporary healthcare staffing refers to the practice of hiring healthcare professionals on a temporary basis to address staffing shortages. In the travel nurse market, registered nurses are hired to work in different locations for short durations. Per diem nurse refers to nurses who work on an as-needed basis, often filling in for sick or absent staff. Locum tenens refers to doctors, usually specialists, who are hired to cover temporary vacancies. The allied healthcare market generally includes various non-physician healthcare professionals, such as medical technologists, occupational or physical therapists, and radiologic technologists, who are hired on a temporary basis.</p></p>
