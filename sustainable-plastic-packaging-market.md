<p><p>Sustainable plastic packaging encompasses two main types: rigid and flexible packaging. Rigid packaging refers to containers such as bottles, jars, and boxes that maintain their shape and structure. It is commonly used for products requiring strong protection and support, like beverages and cosmetics. On the other hand, flexible packaging includes materials like pouches, bags, and films that can adapt to different shapes and sizes. This type of packaging offers convenience, lightweight, and cost efficiency, making it suitable for snacks, fruits, and other lightweight products. Both rigid and flexible packaging play vital roles in reducing environmental impact and promoting sustainability in the plastic packaging industry.</p></p>
