<p><p>DC Distribution Networks market types refer to the different voltage levels at which the network operates. Low Voltage (LV) networks typically operate at voltages up to 1kV, serving domestic and small-scale commercial consumers. Medium Voltage (MV) networks operate at voltages ranging from 1kV to 69kV, supplying larger commercial or industrial consumers. High Voltage (HV) networks operate at voltages above 69kV, providing power transmission over long distances and supplying major industrial consumers. The different market types cater to varying energy demands, infrastructure requirements, and scalability, ensuring efficient and reliable power distribution.</p></p>
