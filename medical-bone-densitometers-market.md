<p><p>Medical bone densitometers are devices used to measure the density of bones, diagnosing conditions like osteoporosis. The market for bone densitometers can be categorized into two types: axial and peripheral. Axial bone densitometers use dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry (DXA) to measure bone density in the spine and hip, providing accurate and reliable results. On the other hand, peripheral bone densitometers use DXA or ultrasound technology to assess bone density in peripheral locations like the wrist, heel, or finger. Both types of densitometers play a crucial role in evaluating bone health and determining the risk of fractures.</p></p>
