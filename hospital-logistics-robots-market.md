<p><p>Hospital logistics robots can be classified into two types: Automated Guided Vehicles (AGVs) and Mobile Robots. AGVs are autonomous vehicles that are programmed to transport goods within a hospital setting, such as medication or equipment, using predefined paths or routes. They typically use sensors and cameras for navigation and can transport heavy loads. Mobile robots, on the other hand, are versatile robots that can navigate independently and perform various tasks, such as delivering supplies, cleaning, or providing patient care assistance. They can easily adapt to changing environments and are equipped with advanced sensors for safe and efficient navigation.</p></p>
