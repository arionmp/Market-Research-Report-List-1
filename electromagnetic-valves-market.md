<p><p>Electromagnetic valves, also known as solenoid valves, are devices that control the flow of liquids or gases through the use of electromagnetic force. They consist of a coil, a movable plunger, and a valve body. When an electric current is applied to the coil, it generates a magnetic field that moves the plunger, allowing or restricting the flow of fluid through the valve. These valves are commonly used in various industries including automotive, oil and gas, water and wastewater treatment, pharmaceuticals, and food and beverages.</p><p>The future outlook of the electromagnetic valves market is promising. <p><p>The electromagnetic valves market includes three main types of valves: direct-acting electromagnetic valves, sub-step direct-acting electromagnetic valves, and pilot-type electromagnetic valves. Direct-acting valves are operated directly by the electromagnetic force, providing a simple and efficient solution. Sub-step direct-acting valves offer the ability to control the opening and closing process in small steps, allowing for precise flow control. Pilot-type valves use a pilot circuit to control the operation of a larger valve, making them suitable for applications requiring high flow rates or pressure.</p></p>
