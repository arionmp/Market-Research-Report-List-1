<p><p>The nuclear density gauge market can be divided into two types: ordinary nuclear density gauge and precision nuclear density gauge. </p><p>An ordinary nuclear density gauge is a device used to measure the density of different materials, such as soil or asphalt, by emitting low-energy gamma rays and measuring their absorption. It is mainly used in construction and civil engineering applications for quality control and compaction monitoring.</p><p>On the other hand, a precision nuclear density gauge is a more advanced version that provides higher accuracy and precision in density measurements. It is typically used in research, laboratory, and scientific applications where precise density data is crucial, such as in geotechnical studies or material testing.</p></p>
